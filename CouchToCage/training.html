COUCH TO CAGE TRAINING

Progressive Phases – From Foundation to Amateur Integration

Phase 1: Foundation

Walking/Jogging in Place

Walking/jogging in place is an effective, joint-friendly indoor cardio workout, great for all fitness levels to build aerobic fitness, burn calories, improve coordination, and engage core/arm muscles, especially by lifting knees, swinging arms, and maintaining good posture for 10-20+ minute sessions.

Duration: 10-20+ minutes
Sets: Warm-up or main cardio
Progression: Increase speed or knee height

HIIT (March Punches)

The HIIT (march punches) exercise is a low-impact cardiovascular movement that combines marching in place with upper-body punching motions. It is often used as part of a warm-up, a beginner-friendly HIIT workout, or a cool-down, engaging both the lower and upper body simultaneously.

Intervals: 20s work / 40s rest
Rounds: 8-12
Total Time: 10-15 minutes

Bodyweight Squats

Bodyweight squats are a fundamental lower-body exercise building strength by mimicking sitting in a chair, involving standing with feet shoulder-width apart, pushing hips back, bending knees, keeping your chest up and back straight, descending until thighs are parallel to the floor (or as low as comfortable), and then driving through heels to return to standing, engaging glutes and core throughout.

Sets: 2-3
Reps: 10-15
Rest: 60-90 seconds

Push-ups

A push-up is a foundational bodyweight exercise that builds upper body strength (chest, shoulders, triceps) by starting in a high plank, lowering your body until your chest nears the floor with elbows at ~45 degrees, then pushing back up, keeping your core tight and body in a straight line from head to heels.

Sets: 2-3
Reps: 8-12 (knee-modified if needed)
Rest: 60-90 seconds

Plank

A plank is an isometric core exercise where you hold your body in a straight, rigid line, like a wooden plank, by supporting your weight on your forearms (or hands) and toes, engaging your abs and glutes to keep your hips from sagging or rising too high, effectively strengthening your entire core, back, and shoulders without equipment.

Duration: 20-45 seconds
Sets: 3
Rest: 45-60 seconds

Static Stretches

Child's Pose: Kneel, sit back on heels, lean forward, arms overhead; hold for 30+ seconds to stretch back and hips.
Standing Quad Stretch: Hold ankle, pull heel to glute, keep knees together; hold 20-30s.
Hamstring Stretch (Seated/Standing): Reach towards toes with straight legs; hold for 20-30s.
Overhead Triceps Stretch: Reach one arm overhead, bend elbow, gently pull with other hand; hold for 30s.
Calf Stretch: Lean against a wall, one foot back, heel down; hold for 30s.

Duration: 20-30 seconds per stretch/side
Sets: 1-2 rounds
Total Time: 5-10 minutes

Breathing Exercises

Breathing exercises, like Belly Breathing, Box Breathing (4-4-4-4), and the 4-7-8 technique, are simple methods to calm the nervous system, reduce stress, and improve focus by controlling inhales, holds, and exhales.

Duration: 5-10 minutes
Cycles: 4-8 per technique
Tip: Practice daily for best results

Expected Health Improvements

Improved cardiovascular health (better endurance, lower resting heart rate); basic strength gains; reduced sedentary risks like weight gain or fatigue. Users often report higher energy and better sleep.

Phase 2: Skill Development

Jump Rope

A jump rope workout is a full-body cardio session, great for burning calories and improving coordination, using various skips like basic, high knees, alternating feet, or criss-cross within HIIT intervals or steady-state cardio.

Duration: 10-20 minutes
Intervals: 30s work / 30s rest or steady
Warm-up: 3-5 minutes easy skip

Jab-Cross Combo

A jab-cross combo is a fundamental boxing move (1-2) starting with a straight lead punch (jab) followed by a straight rear-hand power punch (cross), requiring weight transfer from back to front foot, hip/torso rotation, chin tucked, and hands protecting the face.

Rounds: 4-6 × 3 minutes shadowboxing
Rest: 1 minute
Focus: Rhythm and power

Takedown Defense

Takedown defense in grappling sports focuses on staying on your feet by using head, hands, and hips as defensive lines, primarily through techniques like the sprawl, framing, and controlling wrists/underhooks.

Rounds: 5-8 × 2 minutes drilling
Partner: Recommended
Tip: Drop hips fast, fight hands

Burpees

A burpee is a full-body strength and cardio exercise combining a squat, plank, push-up, and jump. It's highly effective for building strength, improving endurance, and burning calories.

Sets: 3-4
Reps: 8-12
Rest: 60-90 seconds

MMA Yoga Flow

An MMA yoga flow combines mobility, strength, and balance with martial arts-inspired movements like kicks and twists, using poses such as Downward Dog, Lunges, Plank, Cobra, Warrior III, and core twists.

Duration: 15-25 minutes
Flow: Link poses with breath
Focus: Hip openers and rotational power

Expected Health Improvements

Enhanced agility, coordination, and flexibility; stress relief and mental focus from skill mastery; calorie burn for weight management (up to 600-800/session). Builds confidence, reducing anxiety.

Phase 2: Skill Development (Intermediate – Technique & Drilling)

Phase 3: Amateur Integration

Shadowboxing

Shadowboxing is a martial arts exercise where you throw punches and practice movements in the air, as if fighting an imaginary opponent, focusing on form, footwork, and technique without impact. It serves as a warm-up, a way to refine skills, and a cardiovascular workout.

Rounds: 4-6 × 3-5 minutes
Rest: 1 minute
Focus: Combinations, defense, movement

Clinch Work

"Clinch work" refers to grappling and controlling an opponent in close quarters, especially in Muay Thai or MMA, involving tight holds (like a collar tie or double collar tie) for controlling posture, delivering knees, and setting up throws.

Rounds: 4-6 × 3 minutes
Intensity: Technical to moderate
Partner: Required

Ground Basics (Escapes)

"Ground basics (escapes)" refers to fundamental techniques for getting out of disadvantageous positions on the ground in grappling sports like BJJ and wrestling. Core movements for survival and regaining position.

Rounds: 5-8 × 3 minutes positional drilling
Partner: Required
Tip: Bridge, shrimp, frame

Resistance (Dumbbells)

Resistance dumbbells are free weights used for strength training to build muscle and endurance, versatile for exercises like curls, presses, and rows, providing constant resistance.

Sets: 3-4
Reps: 8-12 per exercise
Rest: 60-90 seconds

Foam Rolling

Foam rolling exercises target major muscle groups for recovery and mobility, involving slow rolling and pausing on tight spots (trigger points) for 20-30 seconds to release tension and improve range of motion.

Duration: 10-15 minutes
Areas: Calves, hamstrings, quads, glutes, upper back, lats
Tip: Breathe deeply, no excessive pain

Expected Health Improvements

Full-body strength and endurance boosts; improved mental health via discipline and social ties (if joining classes); overall fitness mimicking athletes, like better body composition. Prepares for amateur events safely.
