Muay Thai: The Art of Eight Limbs

The six best training techniques in Muay Thai focus on developing core combat skills, stamina, and ring generalship.

Shadow Boxing

A fundamental exercise for practicing technique, footwork, and movement patterns without a partner or equipment. It helps build muscle memory and improve stamina and precision in strikes.

Tip: Visualize an opponent and mix offense with defense.

Clinching

This standing grappling technique is essential for controlling an opponent's balance, delivering knees and elbows, and defending against attacks in close range.

Tip: Focus on grip control and off-balancing.

Pad Work

Training with a coach holding Thai pads allows for powerful combinations, timing, and rhythm at high intensity with immediate feedback.

Tip: Explode through the pads with full power.

Sparring

Controlled fighting tests techniques, improves defense, and builds adaptability. Treat it as learning, not winning.

Tip: Stay relaxed and focus on technique over power.

Heavy Bag Work

Vital for developing power, speed, and conditioning for all strikes at full power.

Tip: Treat the bag like a moving opponent.

Jump Rope

Builds cardiovascular fitness, coordination, and footwork rhythm essential for ring movement.

Tip: Keep elbows tight and bounce lightly on the balls of your feet.

Jab/Cross

Fundamental long-range combo focusing on fluidity, balance, and hip-driven power.

Roundhouse Kick

Hip-driven strike using the shin for rotational power to legs, body, or head.

Teep (Push Kick)

Versatile for distance management and disrupting rhythm with thrusting hip power.

Low Kick

Powerful thigh-targeting strike using shin rotation to damage mobility.

Elbow Strike

Devastating close-range weapon using hip rotation for cuts and KOs.

Knee Strike

Versatile for offense and defense with hip drive; deadly in clinch and open range.

BJJ: Ground Dominance

Core concepts include guards for defense/offense and distance management. Essential moves cover submissions, control, and escapes.

Armbar from Guard

Leg-based submission hyperextending the elbow from guard positions.

Triangle Choke

Figure-four leg lock choking blood flow, typically from guard.

Rear Naked Choke

Blood choke from back mount; highly effective finish.

Mount Control

Dominant top position using pressure and leverage to prevent escapes.

Ground-and-Pound

Striking from dominant positions while maintaining control.

Wrestling: The Bridge

Focus on stance, explosive power, control, and transitions between standing and ground.

Wrestling Stance

Low center of gravity with bent knees for balance and attacks.

Double Leg Takedown

Shoot in to grab both legs and drive opponent to mat.

Single Leg Takedown

Attack one leg with leverage and drive to finish.

Sprawl (Defense)

Counter takedowns by dropping hips and sprawling legs back.

Guard Pass

Break past opponent's guard to dominant side control.

Side Control

Dominant position for pressure, transitions, and attacks.
