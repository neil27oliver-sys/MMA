UFC Rules Basics: Welcome to the Octagon

The Unified Rules of Mixed Martial Arts govern professional MMA in the UFC. Understanding them helps you appreciate the strategy, safety, and excitement of every fight.

  • Rounds: Non-title fights are 3 rounds of 5 minutes each. Championship and main events are 5 rounds of 5 minutes. 1-minute rest between rounds.
  • Ways to Win: KO/TKO (referee stoppage), Submission (tap out), or Decision (judges score using the 10-point must system based on effective striking/grappling, aggression, and octagon control).
  • Major Fouls: Eye gouging, groin strikes, headbutts, strikes to the spine or back of the head, small joint manipulation, hair pulling, biting, fish-hooking, and more. Fouls can lead to point deductions or disqualification.
  • Grounded Fighter Rule: A fighter is considered "grounded" if any body part other than hands or feet touches the canvas. This protects them from knees or kicks to the head.
  • Recently Allowed: 12-6 elbows (straight down) are now legal under the current Unified Rules.
